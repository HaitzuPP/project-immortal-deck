A Two-Sided Miner Market

Decentralised video annotation on Bittensor: one pool produces labels, a second pool verifies them, and validators only orchestrate.
Investor briefing · June 2026
The Core Problem
You cannot verify what you are buying, so the design must anchor on the one un-gameable signal
The verification asymmetryIf you could fully check every label, you would not pay for it. Quality can only be judged by proxies.
Miners game the proxyThey optimise for emissions and will exploit whatever signal is rewarded, so the proxy is the design.
The reframeIt is one un-gameable anchor, not five equal layers. Cheaper signals must earn their trust.
Make the anchor cheap enough to use densely, and force every cheaper signal to earn trust by agreeing with it.

Verification in action
Each check is concrete and mostly objective
Deterministic gate73% coverage or a 0.4s segment is auto-rejected before any checker sees it.
Boundary IoU (±0.5s)2.4 / 2.6 = 0.92 passes; 2.4 / 3.8 = 0.63 fails the tolerance.
Canonicalisation"crack egg" and "breaking the egg open" snap to one entry; "prep" fails.
Annotation-goldA miner labelling "idle" on a known clip is caught and downgraded.
Verdict-goldA checker who accepts a planted-defective clip loses reliability.
Weighted consensus(0.95·0.40 + 0.70·0.80 + 0.15·0.95) / 1.80 = 0.60; the weak checker barely counts.
Held-out evalSegment mAP 0.41 → 0.58 accepts the batch; garbage would not lift it.
The division of labourObjective checks are free hard gates; gold grades producers; verdict-gold grades checkers.
The held-out eval is the customer’s un-gameable backstop, measured on model-performance delta, not label count.

Supply side
Who we aim to attract: a competitive spectrum, because we pay for output, not method
Model-builder miners (Pool A)Train video segmentation and captioning models to auto-annotate at scale and near-zero marginal cost. The throughput and cost engine.
Hybrid miners (Pool A)Model-first with a human workforce on the hard tail. Tend to lead the accuracy frontier early.
Human-workforce miners (Pool A)Existing labelling and BPO shops wrapping their workforce as a miner for a new demand channel.
Verification miners (Pool B)Build QA tooling and eval models, with human adjudication for reliability. Verification automates well.
The alpha subsidy rewards automation. At launch, seed a few anchor miners on both sides to populate the two-sided market.

The Thesis
Make the un-gameable anchor cheap enough to use densely, make every cheaper signal earn trust by agreeing with it, and pay only for accepted, verified work backed by real client demand.
That is what separates a defensible annotation subnet from one that pays miners to produce plausible-looking garbage.
